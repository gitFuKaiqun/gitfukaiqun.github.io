Dissertation

Spatiotemporal Event Prediction and Analysis with Urban Data

Information extraction and knowledge discovery from the urban environment is attracting increasing research interest. Ubiquitous sensors and abundant statistical reports provide enormous amount of heterogeneous urban data, such as road traffic data, crime report statistics, social media posts, and imagery of urban streets. With these types of heterogeneous urban data, wide opportunities of valuable and meaningful studies and inferences can be deducted.

The vast deployment of transportation traffic speed sensors and Traffic Incident Management Systems (TIMS) make the traffic speed data and traffic incident records ubiquitously accessible for the transportation operators. With the abundance of the traffic data sources, an efficient multi-task learning model can be implemented to provide an accurate prediction on incident duration.
Fu, Kaiqun. (2020). Spatiotemporal Event Prediction and Analysis with Urban Data (Doctoral dissertation).
